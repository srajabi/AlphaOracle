# Macro Strategy Update: March 1, 2026

## Macro View Alignment
The current regime is "Bull Quiet" on March 1, 2026. This aligns with our "November to April: Historically the strongest six months" seasonality heuristic for U.S. markets. However, the S\&P 500 (SPY) is showing consolidation, with its close price slightly below its 20-day and 50-day moving averages and a neutral RSI (48.46). This suggests that while the broader market might be quiet, specific sector rotations are underway.

The VIX is elevated (19.86) compared to its recent moving averages (SMA_20: 19.15, SMA_50: 17.02), and its MACD is negative on the histogram, indicating a cooling in volatility. This moderate VIX level, combined with the "Bull Quiet" regime, suggests underlying caution but no immediate panic.

Critically, the bond market (TLT) is exhibiting significant strength, with its close (90.82) well above all moving averages (SMA_20, SMA_50, SMA_200) and an overbought RSI (71.61). The MACD histogram for TLT is strongly positive (0.177), signaling robust upward momentum. This strong bond performance implies that the market is pricing in either falling interest rates or a flight to safety (though the VIX doesn't fully support the latter). Our macro view states, "In periods of falling rates, prefer growth and technology." This will be a key dynamic to monitor against current equity performance.

As 2026 is a Midterm year, our Presidential Cycle rule flags potential weakness and volatility during the summer (Q2-Q3). While we are currently in a seasonally strong period (March), this is a forward-looking caution.
