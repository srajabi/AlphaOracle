**Market Regime:** Bull Quiet (but structurally fragile)

- **Bearish/Inflationary Shock:** The U.S. blockade (effective 13 April) removes ~10M bpd, with ANZ raising Brent target to $90. This is a classic supply shock: inflationary (hits consumer spending power) and risk-off (impairs global growth).

- **Sector Impacts:**
  - **Energy (XLE):** Should be primary beneficiary, but ETF shows weak momentum (RSI 44.5, below key SMAs). Market is pricing in a resolution, not sustained disruption. *Divergence to watch.*

**Second-Order Effect:** If blockade persists >2 weeks, inflation expectations reset higher → forces Fed to remain hawkish → compress equity multiples. Energy stocks would catch a powerful bid while tech corrects.

**China-Taiwan Tensions:** Underlying structural risk persists. Any escalation hits the semiconductor supply chain (TSM, NVDA, AMD) and benefits domestic alternatives (INTC) and true havens (GLD, VIX).

**Market Dissonance:** Despite this, long-dated bonds (TLT) are flat, and growth stocks rally. This suggests either:
1. The market believes the Fed will be forced to cut by deteriorating data (recession signals), or
2. This is a bear market rally in a longer-term topping process.

**Softer PPI Data:** The 14 April PPI print crushed dollar demand (GBP/USD rallied). This provided a temporary relief rally but doesn't alter the Fed's strategic patience.

**Duration Trade:** TLT is trapped. It's not pricing in recession (would rally) nor persistent inflation (would sell off). It's waiting for a clearer macro catalyst. **Avoid directional bets here.**

### **1. Geopolitical Landscape: Hormuz Tensions & Iran Talks - A Tug-of-War**

The Strait of Hormuz situation remains the most volatile and impactful geopolitical factor.

*   **Conflict Scenario (Risk-Off, Inflationary):** The "US Energy Secretary Warns of Oil Price Surge Until Shipping Resumes in Strait of Hormuz" and news of "OPEC Crude Output Drops as Iran War Chokes Exports" directly point to a significant supply shock. This implies sustained high oil prices, reinforcing inflationary pressures.
    *   **Impact:** Bullish **XLE (Energy)** and individual energy plays (CEG, TLN). Bullish safe-haven assets like **GLD/IAU (Gold)**. Bearish for long-duration bonds like **TLT**, and potentially negative for broad equities (SPY, QQQ) due to increased input costs and inflation.

    *   **Impact:** Bullish broader equities (SPY, QQQ, VOO), potentially bearish XLE, and supportive of TLT if inflation fears abate.
*   **Second-Order Effects:** The oscillation between these two scenarios creates significant intraday and intra-week volatility. European markets (VGK) are particularly sensitive.

**Strategy:** Maintain core exposure to Energy (XLE, CEG, TLN) as a hedge against continued conflict and inflation.
