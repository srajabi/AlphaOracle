### Geopolitical Events: The Dominant Catalyst

#### Strait of Hormuz / Iran War
- **Headline facts:** Oil prices have **returned to prewar levels** after four months. Tanker traffic rebounds. Iran strike on vessel was the last escalation; now cease-fire and evacuation plan are moving forward. Trump even eases pressure on Fed, implying war relief.
- **First-order effects:**  
  - Oil (XLE) down ~$20/bbl from war peak → XLE technicals (RSI 40, MACD bearish) confirm selling.  
  - Inflation expectations cool → bond yields decline (TLT up, TNX 4.45% and falling).  
  - Safe-haven demand for gold softens (GLD down, massive downtrend).  
- **Second-order effects:**  
  - **Airlines, transportation, and consumer discretionary benefit** from lower fuel costs — not directly in our watchlist but relevant for sector allocation.  
  - **Fed policy room improves** — if oil stays low, inflation may fall from 4.2%, reducing pressure to hike. The “policy cornered” narrative weakens.  
  - **Risk appetite returns** toward cyclical/value — we see XLI (industrials) and XLF (financials) rising, while tech (XLK) is mixed.  
  - **Dollar strength** (UUP at 28.48) may persist as risk-off unwinds but oil collapse reduces terms-of-trade advantage → dollar could eventually weaken. Watch for a pullback.

#### China Trade / Taiwan Tensions
- **Headline:** China links US compliance filings to export restrictions, adds US firms to export control list. Taiwan mulls AI chip export curbs.  
- **First-order effect:**  
  - Semiconductor supply chain risk elevated — TSM, NVDA, AMD, KLAC all impacted.  
  - Trade policy uncertainty adds to inflation floor via tariffs.  
- **Second-order effect:**  
  - **INTC (foundry turnaround) benefits** from Big Tech desire to diversify away from Taiwan. We saw a mention of “Big Tech’s Quiet Diversification Out of Taiwan” as catalyst for Intel.  
  - **Hyperscaler capex** (MSFT, AMZN, GOOGL) may accelerate onshoring — benefits data center infrastructure plays (CEG, TLN, MTZ, NBIS).  
  - **Rare earth controls** by China could boost materials sector (XLB) and defense-related.  
- **Status:** Ongoing and structural; not a sudden shock today but a creeping risk. No immediate regime change.
