## Macro Assessment: A Regime in Transition – Peace Tailwind vs. Fed Headwind

### 1. Geopolitical Pivot: From War to Peace
- **US-Iran framework signed** (14-point MOU) – the single most dominant catalyst of the day. Oil crashes to pre-war levels (~$80/bbl), XLE down ~10% from 20d SMA. The Strait of Hormuz will re-open, but normalisation takes months (experts).  
- **Second-order effects:**  
  - **Falling oil → disinflationary impulse** – reduces headline CPI pressure, gives the Fed marginally more room.  
  - **Airlines/consumers benefit** – lower fuel costs boost margins and disposable income.  
  - **Energy sector (XLE) may stabilise** after the initial flush, but structural supply (UAE ramp, Iranian return) keeps a lid on prices.  
  - **Risk-on rotation**: relief rally already priced in pre-market (SPY futures +0.4%), but VIX only dropped to 18.4 – not a full all-clear.

### 2. Fed & Rates: Hawkish Hold, Yield Curve Warning
- **FOMC held rates**, but more officials see hikes as the next move (median dot shifted). Warsh removed cutting bias and launched a sweeping review.  
- **Market reaction**: 10y yield ~4.49%, 2y yield rose sharply → curve flattening. Bonds (TLT) actually rallied, which is odd – likely a safe-haven bid on war uncertainty still lingering.  
- **Interpretation**: The Fed is cornered – can’t cut into 4.2% CPI, can’t hike into a fragile post-war economy. **Rates will remain higher for longer**, which favours short-duration bonds and value/quality equities over long-duration tech.

### 3. Dollar & Commodities: Strong Dollar, Weak Gold
- **Dollar (UUP) at one-year high**, momentum strongly positive.  
- **Gold (GLD) broke below its 50d SMA**, RSI 38, well into downtrend. The dual forces of a strong dollar and falling inflation expectations (oil down) are crushing the safe-haven bid.  
- **Silver (SLV) and energy (XLE) both deeply negative** – the commodity complex is in a clear risk-off rotation *despite* the equity rally. This is a classic **disinflationary/deflationary signal**: real rates are rising, dollar strong, commodities weak.
