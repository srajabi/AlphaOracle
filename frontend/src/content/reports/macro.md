### 1. GEOPOLITICAL: IRAN/HORMUZ DETENTE vs. STICKY ENERGY  

**First-Order Events**  
- Multiple supertankers have exited the Strait of Hormuz (May 20–22).  
- Trump signals Iran talks are in “final stages”; oil drops ~6% from recent peaks, WTI ~$98.75.  
- Indonesia tightens commodity export controls (supply-chain friction).  

**Second-Order Effects**  

| Trigger | Direct Impact | Second-Order |
|---------|---------------|--------------|
| Hormuz re-opening | Oil supply fears ease → crude rallies pause | **Airlines, consumer discretionary** benefit from lower fuel costs; **inflation expectations** edge down → relief for **bonds (TLT)**. |
| Indonesia commodity controls | Potential supply shocks in nickel, palm oil, etc. | **Materials sector (XLB)** pressured; **inflation** may stay stickier than the oil drop suggests. |
| War-risk premium fading | **Gold (GLD)** loses safe-haven bid, USD softens slightly | Gold may consolidate near $415; **central bank buying** provides a floor. |

**Implication**  
- The oil pullback reduces immediate stagflation risk, supporting risk assets.  
- However, the Indonesia move reminds us that commodity nationalism is rising — **inflation won't collapse**.  
- **Energy (XLE)** remains a strategic hedge but may correct short-term. Look to buy dips, not chase.

### 2. FED POLICY: WARSHAW SWORN IN — RATE CUT HOPES vs. MARKET REALITY  

**First-Order Events**  
- Kevin Warsh is sworn in as Fed Chair; Trump openly seeks rate cuts.  
- WSJ: “Markets Are Bracing for the Opposite” — bond market signals rates are too low.  
- Fed rhetoric continues to focus on rising inflation threat.  

**Second-Order Effects**  

| Trigger | Direct Impact | Second-Order |
|---------|---------------|--------------|
| Warsh expected to be hawkish | **Short-end yields rise** (2Y up); **dollar (UUP)** strengthens on rate differentials. | **Growth stocks (QQQ, NVDA, TSLA)** face valuation headwinds from higher discount rates. **Banks (XLF)** benefit from steeper curve. |
| Trump intervention threat | Policy uncertainty → risk premium in **bonds (TLT)** increases. | **Duration trades (TMF)** remain unattractive; prefer floating-rate or short-dated credit. |
| “Rising Interest Rates are Exploding the Debt” | Fiscal dominance narrative resurfaced. | **Long-term yields** could spike further if markets demand higher term premium. **Stagflation tail risk** rises. |

**Implication**  
- The market is pricing a more aggressive Fed than Trump wants. That tension creates **two-way volatility**.  
- **Sector rotation** from Tech to Financials/Value may accelerate if yields keep climbing.  
- **Cash-secured puts** on quality names (e.g., AAPL, AMZN) remain attractive — we collect premium while waiting for a better entry.
