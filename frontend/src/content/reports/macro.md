### 5. Second-Order Effects & Regime Assessment

**Hormuz → Energy → Inflation → Fed → Bonds → Equity Valuation**
- A sustained oil disruption (~$95/bbl) adds ~0.5% to headline CPI. This would delay Fed cuts further, potentially into 2027.
- **Rising real yields** would compress equity multiples, especially for unprofitable growth (AI startups, high-multiple SaaS). But hyperscalers (MSFT, GOOGL, AMZN) have pricing power and capex flexibility.
- **Tipping point:** If oil crosses $100, expect a sharp risk-off event — sell tech, buy gold, buy TLT (as recession hedge, not as inflation hedge).

**Taiwan → Semi Supply Chain → Foundry Onshoring → INTC, memory, storage**
- Any escalation would crush TSM ADR and spill over to NVDA, AMD. This would trigger a **sector rotation within tech** — away from pure-play AI chip makers toward onshoring beneficiaries (INTC, MU, STX, WDC).
- The "Taiwan chip disaster" narrative (NYT) suggests this is a known unknown — markets may have **underpriced** the risk given the semi index's historic run.

**Regime Change vs. Rotation vs. Positioning**
- **Not a regime change** (no structural break in inflation/growth regime). But the "Bull Quiet" label is brittle.
- **Sector rotation may be imminent** — tech is overbought, energy is oversold, and defensives are neglected. A ceasefire could trigger: oil down → XLE down further? Or oil down → tech up? Historically, oil weakness boosts consumer stocks and airlines but also reduces inflation fear, helping growth.
- **Positioning adjustments recommended:**
  - **Take profits on tech/semi exposure** — at least partial, given overbought conditions and Taiwan risk.
  - **Add international equity hedge** (VGK, VXUS) — outperforming US and less exposed to Middle East tail risk.
  - **Maintain a gold core** (10-15% of liquid assets) — insurance against escalation.
  - **Avoid long bonds** until either oil collapses or recession signals appear.
  - **Prepare for VIX spike** — consider a small VIX call or tail hedge (e.g., OTM SPY puts) as cheap insurance.

**Key risk:** The market is ignoring the possibility that the Iran ceasefire breaks down entirely, leading to a full Hormuz blockade. In that scenario, all risk assets would sell off, and cash/gold/TLT would rally. The current Bull Quiet regime is a **disconnect between calm price action and elevated macro uncertainty.** This is a classic setup for a volatility expansion.
