*   **Overbought Concerns:** Many of the "Long" and "Cash-Secured Put" ideas are on tickers (AMD, AMZN, QQQ, AVGO, SPY) that are showing strong overbought RSI conditions (above 70, AMD especially above 80) or are trading near/above their upper Bollinger Bands. This suggests that while the underlying trends are strong, short-term pullbacks (mean reversion) are highly probable and could pose a risk to outright long option plays or put-selling strategies with insufficient buffer.
*   **Discrepancy in Price:** The `current_price` in the options ideas is consistently higher than the `close` price in the market data for most tickers, indicating that these assets have experienced a rally *since* the market data's closing snapshot. This reinforces the bullish momentum in the very short term but also compounds the overbought concern for some.
*   **Implied Volatility (IV):** IVs vary across tickers. Tech/semiconductor names (AMD, AVGO, CRWD, AMZN) have higher IVs, which is beneficial for selling options (cash-secured puts) but makes long options more expensive. ETFs (GLD, QQQ, SPY) have relatively lower IVs, making long calls or puts cheaper to initiate.
*   **Spread %:** While not explicitly asked for, some contracts have wider bid-ask spreads, especially AVGO and CRWD puts (12-13%), which can impact entry/exit efficiency.

Overall, the proposed strategies lean into **trend continuation** where technicals are bullish, with a few recognizing potential **mean reversion bounces** (GLD calls, CEG puts) or potential **mean reversion pullbacks** (QQQ/SPY long puts as a hedge). The primary technical risk factor across several bullish ideas is the extended nature of the rallies, as indicated by overbought RSIs and positions relative to Bollinger Bands.
