## What the evidence says

- **Taiwan (complete national audit trail, 1992-2006):** ~80% of day traders
  lose money in any six-month period. Fewer than 1% are PERSISTENTLY
  profitable net of fees. The skilled tail is real but tiny - and earns most
  of its profit from the unskilled flow.
- **Brazil (all futures day traders 2013-2015):** of those who persisted
  300+ sessions, **97% lost money**, only 1.1% earned more than minimum
  wage. Persistence does not improve outcomes - traders do not "learn their
  way" to profitability.
- **Why course-sellers can still point at winners:** survivorship +
  short-window luck. In any 6 months, plenty are up; almost nobody stays up.

## So how do the profitable ones actually make money?

The persistent winners in the data are effectively running institutional
mechanics, not directional bets:

1. **Spread capture / market making** - being the passive side, earning the
   bid-ask (Avellaneda-Stoikov formalizes it; needs queue position + rebates).
2. **Speed** - latency arbitrage on stale quotes (HFT infrastructure).
3. **Order-flow information** - seeing/inferring flow before price adjusts
   (payment for order flow exists because flow IS the signal).
4. **Structural edges** - index add/delete front-running, ETF arb,
   closing-auction imbalances - capacity-limited, arms-race niches.
5. **The counterparty:** the 97-99% of retail directional day traders.

None of these are "read charts better." All need infrastructure, scale, or
information advantages retail does not have.

## AlphaOracle verdict

Day trading as practiced by retail is a NEGATIVE-edge activity per the best
available data - it is not in scope and never will be. What we keep from
the literature: the structural mechanisms (overnight premium - already

for every strategy in the lab.
