Executive Summary

The environment is defensive‑leaning with elevated tail risk. Despite a “Bull Quiet” regime label, our intermarket indicators read Transitional: risk sentiment is cautious (VIX rising to 18.8), the dollar is strengthening, real rates are rising, and commodities are directionally split (energy surging, precious metals falling). The rule‑based signals confirm this – slow channel is risk‑on, but the canary is half‑defensive (triggered by TLT negative momentum), and the fast channel / credit channels are clear for now (no sudden stress).

Key tension: Geopolitical supply shocks (Hormuz) are pushing energy and inflation higher, while the Fed is cornered – unable to cut into 4.2% CPI, unable to hike into a war‑shocked economy. This “stagflationary drift” supports energy, gold (on real‑rate logic, but gold is actually falling – see cross‑asset section), and defensive sectors, but hurts long‑duration bonds and rate‑sensitive growth stocks.

Second‑order effects dominate:

  • Hormuz + tanker fire → oil up → XLE beneficiary → airlines, consumer discretionary headwind → inflation expectations up → TLT under pressure → rising yields → growth/tech (QQQ) headwind.
  • Taiwan tensions + rare‑earth export restrictions → semiconductor supply chain risk → TSM, NVDA, AMD under pressure; INTC not immune despite foundry narrative.
  • Fed hawkish chatter → dollar strength → gold down despite safe‑haven demand (classic USD‑gold negative correlation dominates).
