Macro Strategy Assessment — 2026-05-18
Regime: Bull Quiet (but under stress)

1. Geopolitical Risk: Oil Supply Shock Dominates

Event: Strait of Hormuz tensions + US letting Russian oil waiver expire → crude surging above $100 (Brent, WTI).
Second-order effects:

  • Inflation impulse: The PCE energy index jumping 11.6% MoM in March is now being reinforced. This pushes headline CPI toward 4%+ and compels the Fed to maintain hawkish posture.
  • Equity impact: Energy (XLE) is the clear beneficiary. Consumer discretionary (XLY, airlines, retail) and industrials with oil sensitivity will face margin pressure.
  • Bond impact: Bond vigilantes are real – the 10Y yield hit ~4.43% and TLT broke below its 200-day (RSI 33). Duration extension is toxic here.
  • Gold paradox: Gold initially rallies on geopolitical fear, but real yields rising and dollar strength are capping gains (GLD RSI 40, below 20/50 SMAs). Gold’s safe-haven role is being undermined – classic “good news is bad news” for gold if rates rise faster.

Watch: If Hormuz risk escalates (e.g., actual blockage), oil could spike toward $130-150, triggering a full risk-off regime shift (bear vol). Currently the market is pricing this as a manageable tail risk, but the duration of high oil is becoming problematic.

2. Fed Policy & Rates: Hawkish Shift Accelerates

Key signals: Yardeni calling for July hike; bond vigilantes active; Powell appointed interim chair; Kevin Warsh inherits a sticky-inflation economy.
Market pricing: Rate-cut expectations are evaporating. The 10Y yield is at a one-year high. TLT and TMF (3x leveraged) are deeply oversold, but catching a falling knife is dangerous – yields can still climb to 4.6-4.7% if oil stays elevated.
Equity leadership: Growth/tech (XLK, QQQ) is starting to weaken after a strong run. The semi selloff (MU, STX, KLAC down) suggests that the “AI premium” is being challenged by rising discount rates.
Small caps (IWM) are particularly sensitive to hawkish Fed – they rose early in the year on rate-cut hopes, but those hopes are now dashed. IWM is already below its 20-day SMA.

3. Cross-Asset Flow: Dollar Strength = Headwind for Everything

  • Dollar (UUP): Strongest since March, driven by rate differentials (Fed hawkish vs. global central banks cutting/slow). DXY above 104.
  • Gold (GLD, IAU): Under pressure from real yields and dollar. Safe-haven demand is being overwhelmed. May continue to drift lower toward $400 (GLD) unless geopolitical risk morph into banking/liquidity crisis.
  • International equities (VGK, EWA, EWC): Underperform as strong dollar hurts USD-based returns and oil shock impacts Europe/Asia more than US (which is net energy producer). This is a reason to stay US-biased currently.

  • Leading: Energy (+1.8% today), Financials (+1.1%) – banks benefit from steepening yield curve.
  • Lagging: Utilities (XLU -0.75%), Real Estate (XLRE -0.54%) – rate-sensitive; Consumer Discretionary (XLY) – oil inflation headwind; Tech (XLK) falling late Monday.
  • Defensive pockets: Healthcare (XLV) flat; Consumer Staples (XLP) gaining on safety flows.

Implied portfolio action:

  • Overweight Energy (XLE), underweight Long-Duration bonds (TLT, TMF).
  • Cautious on Tech ahead of NVDA earnings on Wednesday – avoid adding until after the event.
  • Consider adding defensive sectors (XLP, XLV) if oil continues to spike.
