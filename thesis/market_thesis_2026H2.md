## Factor theses

1. **Trump factor**: policy is pro-nominal-growth, inflation-tolerant
   ("I love the inflation"), tariff-structural. Net: higher inflation
   floor, weaker Fed independence optics, episodic headline vol. Favors
   real assets (gold, energy) over long-duration bonds. We treat it as a
   PERSISTENT REGIME FEATURE, not an event to time.
2. **Iran factor**: binary and untimeable. Escalation = oil spike + CPI
   shock + risk-off; resolution = a relief rally Trump will claim deflates
   prices "like a rock". Both tails live. Hedge = our defensive sleeves
   already hold GLD/XLE exposure via rotation; do not directionally bet a
   war headline.
3. **AI capex cycle**: the 1999 rhyme is real but incomplete - unlike 1999,
   today's spenders are the most profitable companies in history funding
   capex from cash flow. The bubble question is really a RETURNS-ON-CAPEX
   question that resolves in earnings, not headlines. The danger window is
   when capex growth decelerates (2027 guide: +20% vs 2026's +83% - the
   second derivative has already turned). Watch hyperscaler capex GUIDANCE,
   not AI sentiment.
4. **Inflation**: 4.2% with the Fed on hold = negative real-rate drift =
   structurally good for gold (our canary strategies are already long GLD
   much of the time), bad for long bonds (TLT defense remains suspect -
   the 2022 lesson; smart_defense's adaptive defense matters).
5. **Yen carry**: the single most DATEABLE risk (June 15-16). Hike is
   priced; hawkish guidance is not. $500B exposure means forced
   deleveraging hits the most-owned assets first (mega-cap tech, crypto).
   Aug-2024 showed the shape: violent, fast, 1-2 weeks, then stabilization
   IF fundamentals hold.
6. **IPO liquidity drain**: $75B+ withdrawn from buying power in one week,
   into a market where breadth is already narrow. Late-cycle marker AND
   mechanical near-term headwind.

## Scenarios (12-month horizon, stated 2026-06-12)

- **A. Grind-with-violence (50%)**: no implosion; range-bound to modestly
  up index with repeated 5-10% air pockets (carry unwind tremors, war
  headlines, IPO digestion). Narrow leadership persists until an earnings
  miss breaks it. Our monthly strategies whipsaw a little; canary stays
  mostly risk-on with defensive interludes.
- **B. Slow bear (30%)**: AI capex guidance cuts in Q3-Q4 earnings +
  inflation sticky above 4% + carry unwind = 20-35% drawdown over 2-4
  quarters, 2000-2002-shaped (grinding, not gapping). This is the shape
  our trend/canary strategies handle BEST (GFC-class protection).
- **C. Fast crash (20%)**: simultaneous triggers (BoJ shock + Hormuz
  closure + IPO failure) = 2020-shaped gap event. This is the shape that
  hurts monthly signals; our VIX-term-structure gate (trend_plus_vix_term,
  fires in days) and dual_channel fast brake exist precisely for it.

"Will it implode and when?" - honest answer: B+C sum to ~50% within 12
months, but TIMING is unknowable and we refuse to pretend otherwise. The
edge isn't predicting the date; it's holding strategies that react within
days-to-weeks while others need quarters (see tournament champions).
